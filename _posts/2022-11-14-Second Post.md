Being busy with a heavy semester, I've been re-evaluating my time management lately. I've been thinking about my realtionship with time more generally, too. I listened to an NPR podcast lifekit on the subject and I related to constantly seeing time as a resource meant to be optimized. I spend a lot of my time worrying about whether I will get all my assignments done on time. 

I wonder if it would be better if I just forgot that time was important. If I spent all my time "in the moment." Problem with that is that I might regret not cherishing time better. Not having made more drastic leaps of fate? Never having gone outside the routine. How much do I regret how I've spent my time up until now? I hardly ever think about it. Will I later? 

I feel like at any given moment I should be doing one of three things: working, giving my mind a break, or focused-attention fun. Yes, there are other responsibilities, but that either is counted in "working" or maybe this is just for what I consider "free time". I don't know how consciously I want to decide among all those options. 

For that reason above, I'm choosing to post SOMETHING everytime I sit down to write. This blog will be a living document; articles will change. It's mostly for fun anyway, and it's more personally rewarding to see something get posted for me personally. 

Now anyway, I'm deciding to try to write more. Doesn't really matter about what. Point is to chase interesting ideas and not let them go. Something I made up called intellectual integrity. I don't claim to be original; and I often rely on words of wisdom from those I personally trust. I often apply their advice out of context and you might say "oh that's not a logical thing to do." Why or why is that not a good idea and how could it be described as a probabalistic approach? Probabilstic in the sense that you're not sure. That it's a sudoku puzzle that you can't derive any more without guessing and checking. I have no reasonable model about how to best live my life in the determanistic sense. Heuristics are the way to go. It feels mildly bayseian. 

To what degree is "I like/trust you" the same as I approve of your decisions? Liking is a judgement. Decisions are behavioral. So unless I like you for aspects specifically beyond your control (some physical appearance, maybe personality to some extent). It's kind of bayesian in that P(I'm you| I do what you do) is somehow related to P(you do what you do|youre you). Nana's stickynotes.
